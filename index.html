About me

About Me

Hi, I’m Manoj Kumar B.R., a wildlife researcher passionate about biodiversity, conservation, and ecological research. With an MSc in Environmental Studies and Conservation Practice and hands-on experience in wildlife surveys, field data collection, and socio-ecological studies, I thrive at the intersection of science and nature.

My journey has taken me from conducting camera trap surveys in North Karnataka's reserve forests to analyzing human-wildlife conflict mitigation strategies with local communities. I’m skilled in qualitative and quantitative research methodologies, GIS mapping, and ecological fieldwork, aiming to contribute to conservation efforts through data-driven insights and community engagement.

Beyond research, I’m committed to expanding knowledge on biodiversity and environmental sustainability. When I’m not in the field, I enjoy exploring new ecological landscapes, learning languages, and diving into conservation-related projects.
